Introduction

Success in the classroom is influenced by a vast array of factors, many of which extend beyond traditional markers like intelligence or effort. Academic achievement is shaped by personal habits, socioeconomic background, and external conditions that impact an individualâ€™s learning experience. Factors such as family income, parental education, sleep patterns, study habits, and screen time play a crucial role in determining academic outcomes. Understanding the impact of these variables helps educators, policymakers, and students make informed decisions to maximize academic potential. The goal of this project is to analyze key metrics of student success. By identifying the factors that predict high academic achievement, we can gain valuable insight into the broader behavioral and environmental influences on education, ultimately contributing to a more inclusive and effective education system.
